**Inferred Scoring Policy:**
Based on analysis of combinations 1-14, the scoring formula appears to be:
**Score = (Accuracy + Recall) / 2 - (False_Positives_per_Hour × 0.2)**

This formula:
- Takes the average of Accuracy and Recall as the base score
- Applies a penalty of 0.2 points for each false positive per hour
- Heavily favors models with zero false positives

**Key Insights:**
- **Top 3 performers**: Combinations 11*, 12, and 5* all have zero false positives
- **Best overall**: Combination 11* with 0.663 score (75.9% accuracy, 51.8% recall, 0 FP/h)
- **High accuracy penalty**: Combination 13* has highest raw accuracy (78.8%) but ranks 11th due to false positives
- **False positive impact**: Models with >1 FP/hour (combinations 28, 35) rank at the bottom despite good accuracy
- **Sweet spot**: 40,000 samples with 20,000-30,000 steps seems optimal for balanced performance
